Claudine's Sisig Recipe

Sisig



Sisig is often served as an appetizer or pulutan (a dish paired with alcoholic beverages) in Filipino cuisine.
You can also serve it with rice as a main dish if you prefer.
Adjust the spiciness to your liking by using more or fewer chili peppers.

Ingredients



  • 1 pound (450g) pork face (jowls), ears, and/or belly, boiled and grilled,then finely chopped
  • 1 piece of medium-sized red onion,finely chopped
  • 2-3 pcs of green or red chili peppers,finely chopped
  • 2-3 cloves garlic,minced
  • 2 tablespoons soy sauce
  • 2 tablespoons calamansi or lime juice (adjust to taste)
  • Salt and pepper to taste
  • 2 tablespoons oil for sautéing
  • 1 raw egg (optional, for a creamy texture)
  • Mayonnaise (optional)
  • Chopped fresh cilantro or spring onions (green parts) for garnish

Steps



  1. Start by boiling the pork face, ears, and/or belly in water until tender. Drain and allow it to cool. Once cooled, you can grill the meat until it's slightly crispy, or you can broil it in the oven for a similar effect. The goal is to get some char and crispiness on the meat. After grilling or broiling, chop the meat finely and set it aside.
  2. In a large skillet or pan, heat the oil over medium-high heat. Add the minced garlic and chopped onions. Sauté until the onions become translucent and fragrant.
  3. Add the chopped grilled pork to the pan. Stir-fry for a few minutes until the meat is heated through and starts to become slightly crispy.
  4. Add the chopped chili peppers and continue to cook for a couple of minutes. If you want your sisig to be less spicy, you can remove the seeds from the chili peppers before chopping.
  5. Pour in the soy sauce and calamansi or lime juice. Mix well to distribute the flavors evenly. You can adjust the amount of soy sauce and calamansi/lime juice to your taste.
  6. If using, crack a raw egg into the pan and quickly stir it into the mixture. This will give the sisig a creamy texture. Continue to cook for another minute or so until the egg is lightly cooked.
  7. Season the sisig with salt and pepper to taste. Keep in mind that soy sauce is already salty, so be cautious with the salt.
  8. Optional: Drizzle some mayonnaise over the sisig before serving for added creaminess. You can also garnish with chopped cilantro or spring onions.
  9. Serve your pork sisig hot, typically on a sizzling plate if you have one, and enjoy!

This recipe serves 4 people
